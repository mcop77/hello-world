Casey Anthony Poem


By MARSHALL COPELAND



An innocent life

Ending way to quick

And a husbandless wife

With a crime all too sick


2-year-old Caylee

Found dead in the woods

Six months later than she should be

Casey wouldn’t care if she could


A three-year-old case

Spanning more than a life

Creating a social media base

Giving the defense much strife


The evidence was poured through

But with most of it circumstantial

Casey denied the charges that brew

And the prosecution returned null


Three fateful years

Led up to the 5th of July

The entire world lent their ears

Casey Anthony will not die


Clean of all charges,

Except lying to police

With Caylee’s killer still at large

Out walks Casey, free.




Marshall Copeland
